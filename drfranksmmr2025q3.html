Q3 Digital Performance Report

Dr. Frank Shallenberger — Strategic Review (Jul-Sept 2025)

1. Overall Growth Metrics (Q3)

Follower Growth

+677% (TikTok Lead)

Strong growth across platforms — TikTok (+677%), Instagram (+42%), YouTube (+23%), Facebook (+67%).

Impressions

+412% (TikTok & FB)

TikTok (+412%) and Facebook (+206%) led growth; Instagram and YouTube more modest.

Interaction Surge

+440% (TikTok Peak)

TikTok saw the biggest interaction surge (+440%), while Instagram (+50%) and YouTube (+350%) also improved.

2. Strengths & Opportunities

Key Strengths

  • TikTok dominance: Explosive follower and engagement growth — now the clinic’s strongest platform.
  • Content Fit: Myth-busting / contrarian health takes resonate across platforms.
  • Video First: Short-form video content drives the majority of impressions and interactions.

Weaknesses & Opportunities

  • Facebook underperformance: Low reach per post and minimal engagement.
  • YouTube watch time: High views but low average watch duration (15–30s drop-off).
  • Instagram Stories: Underutilized, low impressions but high opportunity for community (polls, Q&As, behind-the-scenes).

3. Strategic Recommendations

  1. Double down on TikTok: Scale production of short myth-busting clips (statins, cholesterol, autoimmune disease, diabetes prevention).
  2. Cross-post strategy: Cross-post best TikToks/Reels to Facebook with paid micro-boosts to reach older audience segments.
  3. Improve YouTube retention: Add storytelling hooks, chaptering, and stronger CTAs to keep viewers past the 15–30 second drop-off.
  4. Expand Instagram Stories: Daily engagement tools (polls, quizzes, “Ask Dr. Frank”) to deepen community trust.
  5. Content cadence: Maintain frequent short-form uploads (3–5 per week per platform), but selectively boost only the top 10–20% performers.

4. Detailed Action Plan per Platform

TikTok

Q3 Recap / Insights

+677% followers, +440% interactions. Best-performing platform.

Action Plan

  • Content: Continue myth-busting health topics. Add seasonal relevance.
  • Frequency: 5 videos/week (<60s, with captions).
  • Optimization: Strong hooks in first 3s (“Think cholesterol is bad? Wrong.”). Encourage saves & shares with CTAs (“Save this tip before your next doctor visit.”).
  • Goal (Q4): +800–1,000 new followers/week, sustain viral post frequency.

Instagram

Q3 Recap / Insights

+42% followers, +50% interactions. Reels outperform, Stories underused.

Action Plan

  • Reels: 3–4 times/week, recycling top TikToks with platform-specific edits.
  • Stories: Daily short-form stories (polls, Q&A, behind-the-scenes). Weekly “Dr. Frank Mythbuster” poll.
  • Feed: 1–2/week carousels summarizing video points.
  • Goal (Q4): Increase Story reach by 50%, boost engagement rate to >3%.

YouTube

Q3 Recap / Insights

+22% subscribers. Low average watch duration (15–30s drop-off).

Action Plan

  • Video Strategy: 2 Shorts/week (repurposed TikToks); 1 long-form (5–8 min) educational video/week.
  • Retention Fixes: Stronger opening hooks (question/problem first). Add visual aids/chaptering.
  • Growth: Create curated playlists. Promote top Shorts on TikTok/IG Stories.
  • Goal (Q4): Increase avg view duration to 45s+ for Shorts, 3min+ for long-form.

Facebook

Q3 Recap / Insights

+67% followers. Weak post reach and engagement. Reels are the growth lever.

Action Plan

  • Reels: Upload top 3 TikToks/week. Boost top 1–2 Reels/month with $50–100 micro-ads.
  • Posts: Reduce static post frequency. Weekly infographic summarizing 3–5 myths.
  • Community: Use Facebook Groups to repost video content and start discussions.
  • Goal (Q4): Double reach per post, raise engagement rate by 25%.
